Collect Sample for Analysis

Before sample collection:

  • When taking a sample for Ebola diagnostics, remember to use the appropriate Protection and Clothing , i.e. personal protective equipment (PPE)
    (a few words here on how Ebola spreads and don’t spread)

  • Timing of sample collection: Virus is detectable 3-10 days after symptoms appear. If the onset of symptoms is less than 3 days, a later specimen may be needed to completely rule-out Ebola virus.

During sample collection:

  • Whole blood is used for Ebola diagnostics. At least 4 mL is needed for analysis.

  • For collection , use plastic collection tubes. Do not use glass vials or heparinized tubes. EDTA preservative is best, but SPS, citrate, or clot activator is acceptable.

  • Take the sample and label the vials.

  • Dispose needles and syringes in a puncture-resistant container. Then burn the container in an incinerator or pit for burning. If puncture-resistant containers are not available, use empty water, oil, or bleach bottles made with plastic or other burnable material. Do not use resuable needles and syringes.

  • Do not separate and remove plasma and serum.

  • Immediately store or transport the specimen in 2-8 C or frozen on dry ice or cold-packs.

  • Pack the specimen according to:

  • If you spill blood and/or body fluids , remove bulk spill matter, clean the site, and then disinfecting the site. Use a disinfectant with a higher potency than what is normally required for an enveloped virus. Disinfectants with label claims against non-enveloped viruses are preferred.

  • If you have an accidental needlestick injury :
    • Immerse the exposed site in 70% alcohol for 20- 30 sec, and wash with soap and clean water.
    • Flush the site in running water for 20 - 30 sec.
    • If needed, cover with a dressing.

  • If you have an accidental contact with infectious body fluids :
    • Flush the area in the most appropriate manner with soap and clean water. If a splash occurs in the eye, flush it with clean water.
    • Leave the isolation area and remove the protective clothing as recommended.
    • Take a shower and put on street clothes.

    • After sample collection:

    • Transport : Specimens should be placed in a durable, leak-proof secondary container for transport within a facility. Do not use any pneumatic tube system.

    • Laboratory Waste : Place waste in leak-proof containment. Place this bag in a rigid waste container designed for this use. If available, steam sterilization (autoclave) or incineration as a waste treatment process can inactivate the virus and reduces waste volume.
